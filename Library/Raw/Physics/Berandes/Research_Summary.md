# Jacob Barandes: Stochastic-Quantum Research

This directory contains raw information about Jacob Barandes' research into Stochastic Quantum theory and the Stochastic-Quantum Correspondence.

## Key Papers

### 1. The Stochastic-Quantum Theorem
- **arXiv**: [2309.03085](https://arxiv.org/abs/2309.03085)
- **Publication**: philsci:28122
- **Summary**: Establishes a precise correspondence between indivisible stochastic processes and unitarily evolving quantum systems. Explains the origin of complex numbers and Hilbert spaces in quantum theory from first-principles stochastic laws.

### 2. Quantum Systems as Indivisible Stochastic Processes
- **arXiv**: [2507.21192](https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.21192)
- **Publication**: philsci:26042
- **Summary**: Investigates the conceptual foundations of the stochastic-quantum correspondence, focusing on gauge invariance, dynamical symmetries, and Hilbert-space dilations.

### 3. The Stochastic-Quantum Correspondence
- **arXiv**: [2302.10778](https://arxiv.org/abs/2302.10778)
- **Publication**: Philosophy of Physics 3(1): 8 (2025); philsci:28060
- **Summary**: Argues that quantum systems are a general kind of stochastic process in configuration space. Demotes the wave function to a secondary mathematical tool and provides deflationary accounts of interference and entanglement.

### 4. Quantum Conditional Probabilities and New Measures of Quantum Information
- **arXiv**: [2109.07447](https://arxiv.org/abs/2109.07447)
- **Publication**: Annals of Physics 448 (2023); philsci:19747
- **Authors**: Jacob A. Barandes, David Kagan
- **Summary**: Uses a novel form of quantum conditional probability to define new measures of quantum information and explores their relationship with von Neumann entropy.
