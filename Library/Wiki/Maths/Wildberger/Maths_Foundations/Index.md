# Maths Foundations

This directory contains the primary lecture notes and transcripts for Norman Wildberger's "Math Foundations" series. The series provides a constructive and finite approach to mathematics, critiquing traditional set theory and analysis.

## Sections

- **[01: Arithmetic and Geometry](./01_Arithmetic_and_Geometry.md)**
  Foundations of natural numbers, the Hindu-Arabic system, and the development of the rational number field.

- **[02: Algebra](./02_Algebra.md)**
  Exploration of algebraic laws, polynomials, and structural mathematics from a constructive perspective.

- **[03: Real Numbers and Limits](./03_Real_Numbers_and_Limits.md)**
  A critical examination of the real number system, infinite sequences, and the problematic nature of limits in traditional calculus.

- **[04: Rational Geometry](./04_Rational_Geometry.md)**
  Introduction to rational trigonometry and a coordinate-based approach to geometry that avoids the use of real numbers.

- **[05: Data Structures](./05_Data_Structures.md)**
  Computational foundations, including multisets, graph theory, and the informational resolving of mathematical objects.
