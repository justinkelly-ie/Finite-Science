# 06: Logic Complexity and Reduction

This section addresses the computational challenges of logic, specifically the SAT (Satisfiability) problem, and proposes Boole's original reduction methods as a more efficient alternative to standard modern techniques.

## Lectures

- **[267 Sums of products, or products of sums? Neither!](./267_Sums%20of%20products%2C%20or%20products%20of%20sums%EF%BC%9F%20Neither%21.md)**
  Critiques the inefficiency of modern DNF and CNF expansions and suggests that Boole's original algebraic forms provide a more compact representation.

- **[268 Trying to sidestep the SAT problem](./268_Trying%20to%20sidestep%20the%20SAT%20problem.md)**
  Explores whether Boole's algebraic approach can offer shortcuts or heuristic advantages for solving or avoiding the NP-complete SAT problem.

- **[269 Boole Reduction: A challenge for programmers](./269_Boole%20Reduction%EF%BC%9A%20A%20challenge%20for%20programmers.md)**
  A call to action for developers to implement Boole's reduction algorithms, highlighting their potential for automating logical simplification.
