# 03: Algebra of Boole Foundations

This section introduces the original system developed by George Boole and highlights the crucial distinctions between his "Algebra of Boole" and the modern, simplified "Boolean Algebra."

## Lectures

- **[255 The Algebra of Boole is not Boolean Algebra! (I)](./255_The%20Algebra%20of%20Boole%20is%20not%20Boolean%20Algebra%21%20%28I%29.md)**
  Introduction to Boole's original notation and his treatment of logical classes as numbers, emphasizing that Boole's system was closer to standard numerical algebra than contemporary logic.

- **[256 The Algebra of Boole is not Boolean Algebra! (II)](./256_The%20Algebra%20of%20Boole%20is%20not%20Boolean%20Algebra%21%20%28II%29.md)**
  Deep dive into Boole's algebraic laws, including the idempotent law $x^2 = x$ and how it defines the domain of logic.

- **[257 The Algebra of Boole is not Boolean algebra! (III)](./257_The%20Algebra%20of%20Boole%20is%20not%20Boolean%20algebra%21%20%28III%29.md)**
  Discussion on the "Law of Duality" and the fundamental operations that distinguish Boole's original calculus.
