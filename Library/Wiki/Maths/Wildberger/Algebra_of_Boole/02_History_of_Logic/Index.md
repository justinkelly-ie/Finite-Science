# 02: History of Logic

This section traces the evolution of logical thought from antiquity to the mid-19th century. The lectures cover the transition from verbal syllogistic reasoning to the early attempts at symbolic and algebraic formalization.

## Lectures

- **[251 A brief history of logic: Aristotle and deduction](./251_A%20brief%20history%20of%20logic%EF%BC%9A%20Aristotle%20and%20deduction.md)**
  Explores Aristotelian logic, the square of opposition, and the concept of deduction as the foundation of classical reasoning.

- **[252 A brief history of logic: Stoics and other thinkers](./252_A%20brief%20history%20of%20logic%EF%BC%9A%20Stoics%20and%20other%20thinkers.md)**
  Discusses the Stoic school's contributions to propositional logic and the early development of logical connectives.

- **[253 A brief History of Logic: Medieval and Arabic Logic](./253_A%20brief%20History%20of%20Logic%EF%BC%9A%20Medieval%20and%20Arabic%20Logic.md)**
  Examines the refinement of logical tools during the Middle Ages and the critical contributions of Arabic scholars to the preservation and expansion of logical theory.

- **[254 A brief history of logic: from Leibniz to Boole](./254_A%20brief%20history%20of%20logic%EF%BC%9A%20from%20Leibniz%20to%20Boole.md)**
  Tracks the search for a "Universal Characteristic" by Leibniz and the eventual breakthrough by George Boole in creating a fully functional algebraic system for logic.
