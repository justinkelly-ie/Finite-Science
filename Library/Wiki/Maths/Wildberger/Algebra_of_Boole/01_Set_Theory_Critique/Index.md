# 01: Set Theory Critique

This section contains Norman Wildberger's critiques of modern axiomatic set theory (ZFC) and its role as the foundation of contemporary mathematics. The lectures focus on the logical inconsistencies of the continuum and the historical shift from constructive arithmetic to infinite set theory.

## Lectures

- **[250 Modern "Set Theory" - is it a religious belief system?](./250_Modern%20%EF%BC%82Set%20Theory%EF%BC%82%20-%20is%20it%20a%20religious%20belief%20system%EF%BC%9F.md)**
  An introductory overview of the problematic foundations of set theory. Wildberger argues that mathematics has outsourced its foundational logic to philosophy and abandoned the clarity found in constructive, finite arithmetic. He contrasts the "discrete" nature of computation with the "problematic" nature of the continuous real line.
