# The Algebra of Boole: Comprehensive Index

This index provides a high-level overview of Norman Wildberger's "Algebra of Boole" series within the Math Foundations project. The series re-examines the original work of George Boole to provide a logically sound and computationally efficient foundation for logic.

## Course Sections

- **[01: Set Theory Critique](./01_Set_Theory_Critique/Index.md)**
  An examination of the logical gaps in modern axiomatic set theory and the transition from constructive arithmetic to infinite sets.

- **[02: History of Logic](./02_History_of_Logic/Index.md)**
  The evolution of logical thought from Aristotle and the Stoics through the Middle Ages to the breakthroughs of Leibniz and Boole.

- **[03: Algebra of Boole Foundations](./03_Algebra_of_Boole_Foundations/Index.md)**
  Detailed study of Boole's original 1847/1854 system, highlighting how it differs from contemporary "Boolean Algebra."

- **[04: Logical Operations](./04_Logical_Operations/Index.md)**
  Rigorous algebraic treatment of the 16 binary logical operations and their set-theoretic counterparts.

- **[05: Circuit Analysis](./05_Circuit_Analysis/Index.md)**
  Practical application of Boole's calculus to digital hardware, covering transistors, logic gates, and circuit optimization.

- **[06: Logic Complexity and Reduction](./06_Logic_Complexity_and_Reduction/Index.md)**
  Computational perspectives on the SAT problem and the advantages of Boole's reduction algorithms for logical simplification.

- **[07: Boole-Möbius Transform](./07_Boole_Mobius_Transform/Index.md)**
  Introduction to canonical polynomial forms and the systematic derivation of logical conclusions using algebraic transforms.

- **[08: Algebraic Logic and Syllogisms](./08_Algebraic_Logic_and_Syllogisms/Index.md)**
  Advanced topics including Maxels, partial orders, and the algebraic formalization of classical Aristotelian syllogisms.
