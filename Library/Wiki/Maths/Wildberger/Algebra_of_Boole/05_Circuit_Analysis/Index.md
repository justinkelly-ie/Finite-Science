# 05: Circuit Analysis

This section applies the Algebra of Boole to digital circuit design, demonstrating how algebraic manipulation can simplify hardware architecture and canonical representations of logic gates.

## Lectures

- **[260 Boolean algebra and Shannon's circuit analysis](./260_Boolean%20algebra%20and%20Shannon%27s%20circuit%20analysis.md)**
  History of Claude Shannon's landmark thesis that applied Boolean algebra to relay and switching circuits.

- **[261 Transistors, Logic Gates and Boolean algebra](./261_Transistors%2C%20Logic%20Gates%20and%20Boolean%20algebra.md)**
  Physical implementation of logical operations using transistors and the construction of basic logic gates.

- **[262 How the Algebra of Boole simplifies circuit analysis (I)](./262_How%20the%20Algebra%20of%20Boole%20simplifies%20circuit%20analysis%20%28I%29.md)**
  Introduction to the algebraic simplification of complex circuit diagrams.

- **[263 Canonical forms for logic circuits](./263_Canonical%20forms%20for%20logic%20circuits.md)**
  Discussion of DNF (Disjunctive Normal Form) and CNF (Conjunctive Normal Form) and their roles in circuit standardization.

- **[264 Boole polynumbers and equivalent circuits](./264_Boole%20polynumbers%20and%20equivalent%20circuits.md)**
  Using polynomial representations (polynumbers) to identify and verify equivalent circuit structures.

- **[265 How the Algebra of Boole simplifies circuit analysis (II)](./265_How%20the%20Algebra%20of%20Boole%20simplifies%20circuit%20analysis%20%28II%29.md)**
  Advanced techniques for minimizing gate counts and optimizing signal flow.

- **[266 How the Algebra of Boole simplifies circuit analysis (III)](./266_How%20the%20Algebra%20of%20Boole%20simplifies%20circuit%20analysis%20%28III%29.md)**
  Case studies in multi-level circuit optimization and logical reduction.
