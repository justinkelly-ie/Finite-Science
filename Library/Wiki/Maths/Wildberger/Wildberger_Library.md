# Norman Wildberger Mathematics Library

This library centralizes the research, lecture transcripts, and mathematical formalizations of N.J. Wildberger's constructive approach to mathematics. The collection is organized into three primary series, each focusing on a specific domain of the mathematical foundations.

## Main Series

- **[Math Foundations](./Maths_Foundations/Index.md)**
  The foundational series establishing mathematics from first principles. It covers natural numbers, the Hindu-Arabic system, rational field construction, and critiques of infinite set theory and the continuum.

- **[The Algebra of Boole](./Algebra_of_Boole/Algebra_of_Boole_Index.md)**
  A recovery and extension of George Boole's original algebraic logic. This series covers historical foundations, the 16 logical operations, circuit analysis, and the powerful Boole-Möbius transform.

- **[Box Arithmetic](./Box_Arithmetic/Box_Arithmetic_Index.md)**
  A revolutionary approach to arithmetic and algebra based on multisets (msets). This framework provides the algebraic machinery (Maxels, caret products) required for the formal verification of Rational Chromogeometric Information Theory (RCIT).
