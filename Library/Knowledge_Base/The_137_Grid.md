# The 137-Grid: Logical Substrate of RCIT

## Overview
The **137-Grid** is the fundamental logical and geometric framework underpinning Rational Chromogeometric Information Theory (RCIT). It models the universe not as a continuous manifold, but as a discrete, rational configuration space where physical reality arises from the linear tracking of "Atoms of Information" (Transitions).

Implemented in the [**idris2-rcit**](../../idris2-rcit) project, the 137-Grid replaces classical calculus with **Boole-Möbius Transforms** and **Multiset Algebra**, ensuring that every physical state is machine-verifiable and constructively derived.

## Core Pillars

### 1. Linear Multiset Logic (The Substrate)
Reality is modelled as multisets of transitions (Pixels). Unlike standard sets, multisets allow for the tracking of multiplicity and resource consumption, managed via Idris 2's **Quantitative Type Theory (QTT)**.
- **Pixels**: Basic transitions $[A, B]$ between rational points.
- **Maxels**: Multisets of Pixels representing complex physical entities or states.

### 2. Chromogeometry (The Metric)
Space-time is not a single metric but a interplay of three fundamental rational metrics:
- **Blue Quadrance**: Euclidean distance (Standard Geometry).
- **Red Quadrance**: Relativistic/Minkowski distance (Information Horizon).
- **Green Quadrance**: Galilean distance (Limit of absolute time).
The 137-Grid audits the relationships between these metrics to resolve paradoxes (e.g., where Red exceeds Blue, indicating "Dark" configurations).

### 3. The Indivisible Stochastic Process (ISQT)
Physical evolution is not a wave-function collapse but a **Discrete Settlement**.
- **The Density Maxel**: A multiset of cross-projections $[L_i, R_j]$ representing all possible stochastic correlations.
- **Rational Phase**: Quantum phases are modeled as rational spreads, ensuring evolution remains strictly computable and QTT-compliant.
- **The Temporal Handshake**: "Time" is the informational cost (Leibniz Lag) of reconciling these transitions on the grid.

## The "137" Complexity Bound
The name refers to the fundamental complexity threshold of $2^{137}$ logical operations. 
- **The Latency Wall**: At this threshold, the logical complexity of tracking individual information atoms exceeds the grid's capacity for coherent linear tracking.
- **Classical Settlement**: Beyond this bound, the system undergoes decoherence, "settling" into the statistical approximations of classical physics.
- **The Integral Boundary**: Defined at $N=40$ (for internal grid dimensions) to ensure stability before hitting the Latency Wall.

### 4. Cosmological Constant & Leibniz Debt
The expansion of the 137-Grid is an emergent property of informational residue.
- **Leibniz Debt**: Accumulated metrical residue in the vacuum that has not yet decohered into matter.
- **Expansion Rate (H)**: Formally derived as the ratio of Leibniz Debt to the grid scale.
- **Dark Energy**: The physical manifestation of **Green Metrical Torsion** (torsional residue) exerting pressure on the lattice.

### 5. Variable Alpha & Grid Resolution
The fine-structure constant ($\alpha$) is not a universal constant but a local resolution of the grid.
- **Vacuum Polarization**: Verified as a shielding effect where Leibniz debt reduces the effective grid scale ($S_{eff} = S - \text{debt}$).
- **The BNL Invariant**: Proves that $\alpha = 1 / S_{eff}$ across all energy regimes, maintaining a perfectly self-consistent resolution.
